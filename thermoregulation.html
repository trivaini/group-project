HOMEOSTASIS

Homeostasis is the maintenance of the internal environment of the body. Homeostasis is maintained despite considerable stress or changes to the body.

thermoregulation osmoregulation chemical regulation

Thermoregulation is all about the regulation of energy in the form of heat. The 1st law of thermodyamics is: heat is never lost, only converted. Therefore, energy input (DIET) = energy output (HEAT and WORK) "Work" is defined in this case as muscle movement and metabolic processes. Work can be subdivided in to:

  • 1. Transport work - e.g. the transport of molecules from one side of a membrane to another, or the movement of material in and out of the body.
  • 2. Mechanical work -e.g. intracellular mechanics (using fibres and filaments) or the external mechanics of skeletal muscle movement.
  • 3. Chemical work - this can be further divided in to:
  • a. Synthesis, growth and body maintenance
  • b. Energy storage (long-term of short-term)
